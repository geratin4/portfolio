Learning outcomes

Proffesional skills

Future-oriented organisation:


I am in the process of acquiring the skill of crafting plans and establishing clear goals for project timelines. This applies both to my individual projects and collaborative group efforts.

For the recipe page, our team collaboratively established deadlines to ensure timely completion of specific content on the website.


Creating this plan not only enhanced my understanding of project structuring but also facilitated a more organized approach to following the main idea. Additionally, I discovered the importance of remaining flexible during the planning process to accommodate changes in case of any unforeseen inconveniences


  • UniCooked
  • Mario and Luigi Pizzeria

Investigative problem solving:


By actively seeking guidance through questions and feedback from teachers, I am developing the ability to approach problems with a practical and intelligent mindset.

For the pizzeria project, the stakeholder wanted a modern and smooth ordering system to enhance the restaurant's flow. They also desired an Italian touch on the website.


Collaborating with stakeholders taught me the importance of paying attention to details and meeting specific requirements on a webpage.

Personal leadership:


I strive to motivate myself and my team whenever necessary. I take responsibility for my actions, aiming for optimal results. In my studies, I try to make intelligent choices and actively listen to feedback to improve.

During the pizzeria projects, we consistently provided feedback and assistance, whether it was related to the back end or front end. We discussed areas for improvement as well as acknowledged each other's strengths.


In the pizzeria project I learned to take responsibility when you mess up. On the last day before we had to deliver our final project, I accidentally deleted 30 files or more.

Targeted interaction:


When working on an assignment I always take the stake holder in mind. I always ask questions on what are the requirements, so i know what needs to be done and try to help my group

For the pizzeria project, the stake holder wanted a modern and fluent ordering system, that would help the flow of the restaurant.The stake holder also wanted some Italian heritage on the website.


Working with stake holders learned me to pay attention to details and requirements on a web page.

Trends and developments:


Keep myself updated with the changes happing in the digital experience design and try to learn about them. I use this as inspiration for my study area

Iterative process:


Feedback from users and teachers help me improve my design decisions. My projects always goes through different stages, I always change my designs after the feedbacks so i can meet the needs of the people who will use my product

For UniCooks we decided to let our class mates choose what sort of information they would like to see in different tabs. each one had a different option/ idea.


i learned from doing this that every one has a different style or a different way that they would like to see things in a webpage.


As a team we choose the most common ones and the ones we found will fit better and applied it to our project

Interactive prototypes:


I create digital projects that can be used by people to interact with devices. I start with sketches and prototypes and use technical languages like HTML and CSS to give them structure and style.

we made a paper prototype of Unicooks, We added the features and layout that we wanted to use on our website so users can interact with it. We let our class mate interest with the paper prototype and gave them explanation on our ideas and we asked them on their feedback on how they found it and any improvements/changes we can make.


I learned from this that prototypes are very important in the process of making a website. It is easier to show case the idea to the user/stake holder and they can also interact with it which give them an idea on what the website would be. in the mario & Luigi project, we did not really focus on a paper prototype or even showcasing it to users, to give their feed back.
