Learning outcomes

Professional skills

Future-oriented organization:


I am in the process of acquiring the skill of crafting plans and establishing clear goals for project timelines. This applies both to my individual projects and collaborative group efforts.

For Unicooked, our team collaboratively established deadlines to ensure timely completion of specific content on the website.


Creating this plan not only enhanced my understanding of project structuring but also facilitated a more organized approach to following the main idea. Additionally, I discovered the importance of remaining flexible during the planning process to accommodate changes in case of any unforeseen inconveniences


  • UniCooked
  • Mario and Luigi Pizzeria

Investigative problem solving:


By actively seeking guidance through questions and feedback from teachers, I am developing the ability to approach problems with a practical and intelligent mindset.

For Mario and Luigi Pizzeria, the stakeholder wanted a modern and smooth ordering system to enhance the restaurant's flow. They also desired an Italian touch on the website.


Collaborating with stakeholders taught me the importance of paying attention to details and meeting specific requirements on a webpage.

Personal leadership:


I strive to motivate myself and my team whenever necessary. I take responsibility for my actions, aiming for optimal results. In my studies, I try to make intelligent choices and actively listen to feedback to improve.

During Mario and Luigi Pizzeria, we consistently provided feedback and assistance, whether it was related to the back end or front end. We discussed areas for improvement as well as acknowledged each other's strengths.


One valuable lesson from the pizzeria project was learning to take responsibility for mistakes. On the final day before delivering our project, I unintentionally deleted 30 files or more, highlighting the importance of owning up to errors and finding solutions.

When I work on assignments, I consider the stakeholder's perspective. I ask questions to understand the requirements and help my group.

In Mario and Luigi Pizzeria, the stakeholder aimed for a modern and seamless ordering system to enhance the restaurant's flow. Additionally, they desired an infusion of Italian heritage on the website.


Collaborating with stakeholders taught me the importance of paying attention to details and meeting specific requirements on a webpage.

Technical Skills

Trends and developments:


Working on these projects allowed me to explore various advancements in digital experience design. This experience has enabled me to form opinions about digital experiences, influencing my decision in choosing a study direction.

In Mario and Luigi Pizzeria, I believe I didn't acquire significant experience or knowledge in digital design. However, with UniCooked, I did manage to gain some valuable experience and knowledge in the field of digital design.


In Mario and Luigi Pizzeria, we didn't engage in any research on design; instead, we proceeded directly to creating a web page in HTML following a paper-based design.


In UniCooked, we conducted some research on website design, exploring color schemes and strategies to fit our website to our target audience. Additionally, we experimented with various paper prototypes design concepts, encouraging student interaction to gather feedback that informed our final design decisions.


Observing the distinct outcomes of Mario and Luigi Pizzeria compared to UniCooked taught me the significance of the design process. In the former project, where design was not prioritized, the result lacked visual appeal, highlighting the importance of investing time in the design phase, as evidenced by the more visually pleasing outcome in UniCooked, where emphasis was placed on the design process.

  • Mario and Luigi Pizzeria

Iterative process:


Feedback from users and teachers plays a crucial role in enhancing my design choices. My projects go through various stages, and I consistently modify my designs based on feedback to meet the needs of the users.

For UniCooked, we allowed our classmates to choose the information they wanted in different tabs.Each person had a unique preference.


This experience taught me that everyone has a different style or preference for how they want to see things on a webpage.


As a team, we selected the most common and fitting options, incorporating them into our project.


  • UniCooked
  • Mario and Luigi Pizzeria

Interactive prototypes:


I create digital projects for people to interact with devices, starting with sketches and prototypes. I use technical languages like HTML and CSS for structure and style.

For UniCooks, we made a paper prototype with the features and layout we wanted. We had classmates interact with it, explained our ideas, and gathered feedback for improvements.


This taught me the importance of prototypes in website development. They make it easier to showcase ideas to users and stakeholders, allowing them to interact and understand what the website will be like. In contrast, with the Mario & Luigi project, we didn't focus much on a paper prototype or gathering user feedback.


  • UniCooked
  • Mario and Luigi Pizzeria

Target group:


When working on a project, I always prioritize what the end-users want and need. I take their interests into account, ensuring the product becomes useful for them throughout the development process.

Our primary focus for Unicooked was students. To adapt our website to their needs, we conducted a survey on daily cooking habits. Additionally, we created prototypes for students to interact with, gathering valuable feedback. These efforts collectively contributed to making Unicooked an ideal platform for students.


By doing the survey and creating prototypes, I learned how to use feedback to improve the website. Many of our recipe categories were based on what people shared in the survey.
